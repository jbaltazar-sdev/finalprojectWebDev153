Bobby Shew is an acclaimed American jazz trumpet and flugelhorn player known for his versatility and mastery of both lead and solo performance styles. Shew's career spans decades, during which he has played with iconic bands like the Woody Herman Orchestra, the Buddy Rich Big Band, and Maynard Ferguson's band. In addition to his performance career, Shew is a highly respected educator and clinician, inspiring generations of brass players worldwide.

Cannonball Adderley was a renowned jazz alto saxophonist known for his soulful and energetic playing, blending bebop and soul jazz styles. He played with legends like Miles Davis, contributing to the iconic Kind of Blue album, and led his own bands, performing in venues worldwide.

Wycliffe Gordon is an acclaimed jazz trombonist, composer, and educator known for his virtuosic playing and deep connection to traditional and modern jazz styles. A longtime member of the Wynton Marsalis Septet and the Lincoln Center Jazz Orchestra, he has also performed with legends like Dizzy Gillespie, Tommy Flanagan, and Lionel Hampton. Renowned for his warm tone and engaging stage presence, Gordon has received numerous accolades, including "Trombonist of the Year" from the Jazz Journalists Association multiple times.

Sir James Galway is a world-renowned flutist celebrated for his virtuosic technique and warm, expressive tone, earning him the title "The Man with the Golden Flute." He has performed as a soloist with leading orchestras worldwide, including the Berlin Philharmonic and London Symphony Orchestra, and collaborated with artists like Elton John and Andrea Bocelli. A passionate educator and recording artist, Galway has made classical music accessible to broad audiences through his extensive discography and charismatic performances.
